## Project Overview

This project analyzes the efficiency of the Unaccompanied Alien Children (UAC) care transition pipeline using data from the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS).

The study focuses on how effectively children move through the care system:

CBP Custody → HHS Care → Sponsor Placement

The project identifies operational bottlenecks, evaluates transfer and discharge performance, measures backlog accumulation, and forecasts future process efficiency trends.

- Children Transferred out of CBP Custody
- Children in HHS Care
- Children Discharged from HHS Care

---

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### Transfer Efficiency Ratio

Transfers ÷ Children in CBP Custody

### Discharge Effectiveness

Discharges ÷ Children in HHS Care

### Pipeline Throughput

Discharges ÷ Apprehensions

### Backlog

Apprehensions − Discharges

### Health Score

Composite performance indicator used to monitor overall system effectiveness.

## Key Findings

- Transfer Efficiency remained stable during 2023–2024 but declined significantly during 2025.
- Discharge Effectiveness decreased over time, indicating slower placement outcomes relative to HHS care population.
- Backlog levels generally remained under control and approached equilibrium by 2025.
- Forecasting results suggest transfer efficiency may remain below historical averages without operational intervention.
- Continuous monitoring can help identify emerging bottlenecks before they impact placement outcomes.
